UNSC

Under the United Nations Charter, the SC has primary responsibility for the maintenance of peace and security. It takes the lead in determining the existence of a threat to peace or stability. It does so by calling upon parties to settle disputes by peaceful means and also by authorising military actions, peacekeeping operations, and international sanctions if necessary.

It has 15 Members, and each Member has one vote. Out of these, 5 countries have permanent seats – USA, UK, France, Russia and China. They also hold veto power. The other 10 seats are awarded for a period of 2 years by the General Assembly.

Agenda 1: Global terrorism and its international impact with special attention to the Middle East

As the situation in the Middle East continues to deteriorate, the maintenance of global peace and security becomes all the more difficult in the eyes of the International Community. Mobilisation of troops by various countries around the epicentre of the conflict and beyond has attempted to alleviate the conditions but the outcome hasn’t been constructive enough. In wake of this crisis, it is up to the United Nations Security Council to intervene and reinstate calm by ending the conflict.


Agenda 2: Resolution of the conflict in the Korean Peninsula.

As the status quo in the Korean peninsula worsens, the world at large watches intently while threats of military action are exchanged between the two bordering nations. The Democratic People’s Republic of Korea’s insistence to continue with its development of nuclear weapons and the volatility of the relationship between DPRK and South Korea only serve as the ingredients for a massive calamity. Amidst this situation, the UNSC must act quickly to neutralise the situation, for the benefit of the international community.
