HRC

"All victims of human rights abuses should be able to look to the HRC as a forum and springboard for action". -Ban-Ki Moon, UN Secretary General

The Human Rights Council is an international body responsible for the promotion and protection of human rights around the world and addressing any situations of human rights violations. It is made up of 47 states, elected by the UN General Assembly.

The HRC was created in 2006 to replace the former UN Commission on Human Rights. It has the ability to discuss all thematic human rights issues and situations that require its attention throughout the year.



Agendas

Download Study Guide

Agenda 1: Upholding and guaranteeing rights to refugees and people displaced due to climate change.

The Earth’s climate is changing at a rate that has exceeded most scientific forecasts. Some families and communities have already begun to suffer the adverse effects of this climatic change, forced to leave their homes in search of a new beginning. Temperatures, pollution and water levels seem to rise with every passing year, as do the number of refugees they create. When the water’s edge is at your doorstep, when rainstorms are blowing away rooftops, it isn’t political, it’s personal.


Agenda 2: Measures to protect rights of minorities, such as the Kurds, in the Middle East and North Africa.

From Syria to Iraq to Libya and Yemen, the cultural and religious fabric in the Middle East, intricately woven over centuries has been torn apart by terrorist intents. It is imperative to primarily protect the rights of these minorities, whose culture has given rise to some of the world’s greatest civilisations. Thousands of civilians were at the mercy of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant/Syria (ISIL/ISIS) or Da’eesh, whose propagandists were systematically eradicating ethnic and religious minorities. It is at the crux of the HRC to protect the rights of these minorities who have been the victims of perpetrated genocides, war crimes and violence.
