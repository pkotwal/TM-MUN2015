1965: The situation in Vietnam

Nearly 50 years ago, in the wake of the conflict between North and South Vietnam, 3500 US Marines landed in the region and proceeded to begin a long program of sustained bombing of North Vietnamese targets. They were the first American combat troops on the ground in a conflict that had been building for decades. The United States rapidly increased its military forces in South Vietnam, prompted by the realization that the South Vietnamese government was on the losing side, as the communist-dominated Viet Cong gained influence over much of the population. As a result, North Vietnam also rapidly increased its infiltration of men and supplies to combat South Vietnam and the U.S. Thus began one of the deadliest conflicts in history.

In addition to military bombing, the Vietnam War saw a wide array of defoliating agents, namely Agent Orange and napalm, as part of the United Stateâ€™s herbicidal warfare program. The objective was to remove the jungle cover utilized by the Viet Cong and resulted in disabilities and heath problems of nearly 1 million people.The fatality statistics of the war vary, the estimate of the total dead being between 1.5 to 3.6 million people.
